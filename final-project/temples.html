Get to know the California temples

California is the first state outside of Utah to have multiple dedicated temples, with a total of 12 currently operating. The first temple built in California was the Los Angeles California Temple, dedicated in 1956.

The Los Angeles California Temple was the pioneer of temple construction in California, breaking ground for the first temple built within the state.

After the Salt Lake Temple, the Los Angeles California Temple was the first to feature the iconic angel Moroni statue.

At the time of its construction, the Los Angeles California Temple was the largest temple of the church.

The Los Angeles California Temple was the last temple designed for live acting of the endowment ceremony.

The Los Angeles California Temple also boasts beautiful hand-painted murals in its ordinance rooms, and it is one of only seven temples where patrons progress through four ordinance rooms before entering the Celestial Room.
